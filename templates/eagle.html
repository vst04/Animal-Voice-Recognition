Eagle

Origin and Habitat:

Eagles are large birds of prey belonging to the family Accipitridae. They are found in a wide range of habitats across the globe, including forests, mountains, and near large bodies of water.

Physical Traits:

Eagles are known for their impressive wingspan, sharp talons, and powerful beak. They have keen eyesight, which allows them to spot prey from great distances. Their plumage varies among species but is often a mix of brown, white, and golden hues.

Behavior:

Eagles are carnivorous and primarily hunt fish, small mammals, and other birds. They are solitary hunters but may form monogamous pairs during the breeding season. Eagles are known for their majestic flight and can soar at high altitudes for long periods.

Lifespan:

In the wild, eagles typically live for 20 to 30 years. Some species can live longer in captivity, with lifespans reaching up to 50 years under optimal conditions.
