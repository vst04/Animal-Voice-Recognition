Human

Origin and Habitat:

Humans, or Homo sapiens, are a species of primate that originated in Africa approximately 300,000 years ago. Today, humans inhabit diverse environments across the globe, from urban cities to rural areas, adapting to a wide range of climates and landscapes.

Physical Traits:

Humans are bipedal with an upright posture, allowing for the use of hands for complex tasks. They have a highly developed brain, which enables advanced cognitive abilities, language, and tool use. Human skin color, hair type, and other physical features vary widely among populations.

Behavior:

Humans are highly social beings, forming complex societies and cultures. They engage in a variety of activities including agriculture, industry, art, science, and communication. Humans demonstrate a wide range of emotions and behaviors, and their social structures are influenced by cultural, economic, and political factors.

Lifespan:

The average human lifespan varies by region and lifestyle but typically ranges from 70 to 80 years. Advances in medicine, nutrition, and living conditions have contributed to increased life expectancy over the past century.
